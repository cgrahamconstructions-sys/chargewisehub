    Understanding Charging Technology

    Modern charging is more than just plugging in. Here's what you need to know about today's charging standards.

    USB-C PD (Power Delivery)

    The universal fast-charging standard delivering up to 240W. Powers everything from phones to laptops with intelligent power negotiation.

    GaN (Gallium Nitride)

    Revolutionary semiconductor technology that makes chargers up to 40% smaller while running cooler and more efficiently than traditional silicon.

    Qi & Qi2 Wireless

    Industry-standard wireless charging at 5-15W. Qi2 adds magnetic alignment for faster, more efficient charging on compatible devices.

    MagSafe

    Apple's magnetic wireless charging system for iPhone 12+, delivering up to 15W with perfect alignment every time.

    PPS (Programmable Power Supply)

    Advanced charging protocol that dynamically adjusts voltage for maximum efficiency. Required for Samsung Super Fast Charging.

    Common Questions About Device Chargers

    Answers to the questions Australian buyers ask most.

    What is the best wireless phone charger in Australia?

    For most Australians, the Belkin BoostCharge Pro 3-in-1 is our top recommendation. It charges your iPhone at full 15W MagSafe speed while simultaneously handling your Apple Watch and AirPods—all on one sleek stand.

    If you're on a budget, the Anker MagGo 3-in-1 offers similar functionality with Qi2 certification at a lower price point. Samsung users should look at the Samsung Wireless Charger Duo for optimised Galaxy device charging.

    We've tested all the major options—see our full breakdown in the Best Wireless Phone Chargers Australia guide.

    Are MagSafe chargers worth it?

    Yes, if you have an iPhone 12 or newer. MagSafe's magnetic alignment means you'll never wake up to a phone that slipped off the charger overnight. It also charges at 15W—double the speed of standard Qi wireless chargers.

    The main advantages are convenience (one-handed attachment), faster charging, and the ability to use your phone while it charges. The magnets are strong enough that you can pick up your phone with the charger still attached.

    The downside? MagSafe chargers cost more than standard Qi pads. But for daily use, most iPhone users find the premium worthwhile. Check our wireless charger comparison to see MagSafe options from Apple, Belkin, and Anker.

    What's the best power bank for travel?

    For most travellers, we recommend the Anker 737 PowerCore 24K. With 24,000mAh capacity and 140W output, it can charge your phone 5+ times and even power a MacBook Pro. The smart display shows exactly how much juice is left.

    If you need something more compact, the Anker MagGo 5000mAh snaps onto your iPhone via MagSafe and fits in any pocket. For budget-conscious travellers, the Belkin BoostCharge 20K offers excellent capacity at under $100.

    Important for flying: Most airlines allow power banks up to 100Wh (~27,000mAh) in carry-on only—they're banned from checked luggage. All our top picks are flight-approved.
