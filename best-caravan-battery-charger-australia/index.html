How to Choose the Right Caravan Charger

The right setup depends on how you travel:

  • Mostly caravan parks? A 240V smart charger (Victron Blue Smart or NOCO GENIUS10) is all you need.
  • Mix of parks and free camping? Add a DC-DC charger (REDARC BCDC1225D) to charge while driving between sites.
  • Full off-grid? You need DC-DC + solar MPPT + adequate panel capacity. Budget $600–$1,000 for the complete system.
  • Have a LiFePO4 battery? Make sure every charger in your system has a dedicated LiFePO4 profile — not just "lithium compatible".

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best caravan battery charger in Australia?

For DC-DC (driving), the REDARC BCDC1225D. For 240V at caravan parks, the Victron Blue Smart IP65 25A.

Do I need a DC-DC charger if I have solar?

Both are recommended for off-grid travel. Solar works when stationary and sunny; DC-DC charges while driving. Together they give you reliable power in all conditions.

Can I use a regular battery charger in my caravan?

You can for occasional 240V top-ups, but a proper multi-stage smart charger designed for deep-cycle batteries is much better for your battery's long-term health. If you have a LiFePO4 battery, you must use a lithium-compatible charger.
