Buying Guide — What to Look For

Capacity (mAh)

A modern iPhone 16 has a ~3,600mAh battery. A Samsung Galaxy S25 is around 4,000mAh. So a 10,000mAh power bank gives you roughly 2–2.5 charges (accounting for conversion losses). For laptops, you need 20,000mAh+ and at least 65W output.

Output wattage

For phones: 18–30W is fine. For tablets: 30–45W. For laptops: 65W minimum, 100W+ preferred. If the power bank only outputs 5W, it'll charge your phone slower than it drains during use.

Airline rules in Australia

Qantas and Virgin both follow IATA rules: power banks up to 100Wh (about 27,000mAh at 3.7V) go in carry-on, no approval needed. Check your specific airline for anything above that.
