Why Upgrade From a Standard Power Point?

Most EVs come with a "granny cable" that plugs into a standard 10A Australian power point. It works, but it's painfully slow — typically adding just 10–15km of range per hour.

If you drive 50km per day, you'd need 4–5 hours plugged in every night on a standard socket. A Level 2 charger cuts that to under 2 hours for most EVs.

What is a Level 2 Home Charger?

A Level 2 charger (also called EVSE — Electric Vehicle Supply Equipment) uses a dedicated 32A circuit to deliver 7.4kW–22kW of AC power. It's hardwired by a licensed electrician and uses a Type 2 connector — the Australian standard for EVs.

All major EVs sold in Australia — Tesla, BYD, MG, Hyundai Ioniq, Kia EV6, Volvo, BMW, Mercedes — accept Type 2 charging.

💡 Pro Tip: Always get 3 quotes from licensed electricians. Use a C-class licensed electrician who has experience with EV installations — not just a general electrician. Ask specifically if they've done EV installs before.

Government Rebates & Incentives

Several states offer rebates that can significantly reduce your installation cost:

  • Victoria: Zero Emissions Vehicle (ZEV) Subsidy — up to $3,000 off new EVs, and some council EV charger rebates
  • Queensland: QLD Government EV rebate ($3,000 for eligible new EVs); check with Ergon/Energex for home charging grants
  • NSW: $3,000 EV rebate for vehicles under $68,750; also check Sydney Water/council programs
  • SA: $3,000 EV subsidy for eligible vehicles
  • ACT: Interest-free loans for EVs and home charging equipment via Sustainable Household Scheme

Check energyrating.gov.au and your state energy authority for current rebates — they change frequently.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a licensed electrician to install an EV charger?

Yes — in all Australian states it's a legal requirement. Never DIY an EV charger installation. Hire a C-class licensed electrician with specific EV installation experience.

Can I charge my EV from a normal power point?

Yes, but it's very slow — around 10–15km range per hour. Fine for occasional top-ups but impractical as your primary charging method. A Level 2 charger gives 40–65km per hour.

Will an EV charger increase my home's value?

Evidence is emerging that EV-ready homes sell faster and for more. As EV ownership grows in Australia, a hardwired charger is increasingly seen as a premium feature — similar to air conditioning.

How long does installation take?

A standard installation with no switchboard upgrades takes 2–4 hours. If a switchboard upgrade is needed, budget a full day.
