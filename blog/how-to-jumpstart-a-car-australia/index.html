How to Jumpstart a Car — Step-by-Step Guide Australia 2026

Dead battery? Here's exactly how to safely jumpstart your car using jumper cables or a portable jump starter.

⚠️ Important: Modern cars with stop-start systems, AGM batteries, or advanced electronics may need a portable jump starter rather than jumper cables. Using cables incorrectly on these vehicles can damage the ECU. Check your owner's manual first.

Method 1: Using a Portable Jump Starter (Recommended)

A portable jump starter (also called a jump pack) is the safest and most convenient method — no donor car needed. Here's how to use one:

  1. 1
    Check the jump starter is charged — most have an LED indicator. A dead jump starter is useless.
  2. 2
    Turn off all accessories in the car — lights, radio, air conditioning.
  3. 3
    Connect the RED clamp to the positive (+) battery terminal first.
  4. 4
    Connect the BLACK clamp to an unpainted metal ground point — not directly to the negative terminal. This prevents sparking near the battery.
  5. 5
    Start the jump starter (some activate automatically when connected).
  6. 6
    Start the car. If it doesn't start within 3 seconds, wait 30 seconds and try again. Don't crank for more than 5 seconds at a time.
  7. 7
    Remove clamps in reverse order — BLACK first, then RED.
  8. 8
    Drive for at least 30 minutes to let the alternator recharge your battery. Better yet, put it on a smart charger overnight.

Method 2: Jumper Cables From Another Car

The classic method — works fine for older vehicles without AGM batteries or advanced electronics.

  1. Park the donor car close but not touching the dead car. Both cars off.
  2. Connect RED to (+) terminal on dead battery, then RED to (+) on donor battery.
  3. Connect BLACK to (-) on donor battery, then BLACK to unpainted metal on dead car (not the (-) terminal).
  4. Start the donor car and let it run for 2–3 minutes.
  5. Try to start the dead car.
  6. Once running, remove cables in reverse: BLACK from dead car, BLACK from donor, RED from donor, RED from dead car.
  7. Drive for 30+ minutes.
