Why Lithium Batteries Need a Different Charger

Lithium batteries (both LiFePO4 and Li-ion) operate on a fundamentally different chemistry to lead-acid batteries. They require:

  • Constant Current / Constant Voltage (CC/CV) charging — not the multi-stage bulk/absorption/float process used for lead-acid
  • No float charging — lithium batteries don't need or want a maintenance float charge. Continuous float voltage degrades lithium cells
  • Precise voltage limits — LiFePO4 charges to 14.4–14.6V max. Exceeding this causes permanent damage
  • No desulfation pulses — standard chargers often send high-voltage pulses to recover lead-acid batteries. These destroy lithium cells

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I charge a LiFePO4 battery with a normal charger?

No — standard chargers damage lithium cells. Always use a charger with a dedicated LiFePO4 mode.

What voltage should I charge a 12V LiFePO4 battery to?

14.4–14.6V maximum. Exceeding this causes permanent damage. A quality smart charger handles this automatically.

What's the difference between LiFePO4 and lithium-ion?

LiFePO4 (lithium iron phosphate) is the safe, long-life lithium chemistry used in caravans, 4WDs and marine. Standard lithium-ion is used in phones and laptops. For 12V vehicle applications, LiFePO4 is almost always what you want.

Do I need a special charger for a lithium caravan battery?

Yes. If your caravan has a LiFePO4 battery, you need a charger with a dedicated LiFePO4 profile. The Victron Blue Smart or NOCO GENIUS10 are the best options.
